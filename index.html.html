Motorsport CAD + FEA Fabrication
Ackermann Steering System — Triton Solar Car
Full design-to-fabrication cycle on the steering geometry for UCSD's American Solar Challenge race car. Led trade study between full and partial Ackermann configurations to minimise tyre scrub and turning radius. Modelled geometry in SolidWorks 2D/3D, validated with ANSYS FEA (55,338 psi worst-case), then fabricated steel and wooden steering plates via CNC waterjet and laser cutting. Assembled and installed the complete steering column and control module on the vehicle.

Rotor Swept Area Effects on Wind Turbine Power Output
Fabricated and tested variable-length 3D-printed rotors to investigate swept-area scaling on electrical power output. Correlated voltage and current measurements across rotor radii, achieving R² = 0.794. Benchmarked experimental efficiency trends against the Betz limit. Results confirmed nonlinear behaviour at low wind speeds consistent with literature.
